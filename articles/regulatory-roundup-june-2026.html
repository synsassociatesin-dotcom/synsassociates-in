Regulatory Update — June 2026

India Regulatory Roundup — June 2026: Key Circulars from RBI, MCA, IBBI, SEBI & IRDAI

June 2026 · 10 min read · Syns Associates Research Team

This edition of the Syns Associates Regulatory Roundup covers significant regulatory developments across RBI, MCA, IBBI, SEBI, and IRDAI issued in June 2026. Businesses, financial institutions, and compliance teams must review the items relevant to their operations and take timely action — several of these carry hard deadlines.

Reserve Bank of India

1. SFB Credit Facilities to InvITs — New Framework (Para 137A)

RBI has issued comprehensive directions governing lending by Small Finance Banks (SFBs) to Infrastructure Investment Trusts (InvITs) under the SFB Credit Facilities — Second Amendment Directions, 2026. A new paragraph 137A has been inserted, creating a structured lending framework for the infrastructure financing ecosystem.

Eligibility Criteria

SFBs may lend only to InvITs that meet all of the following:

  • Listed on a recognised stock exchange and registered with and regulated by SEBI
  • At least 80% of asset value comprises completed, revenue-generating infrastructure projects
  • Underlying projects have demonstrated positive cash flows for a minimum of one year prior to lending

Structural Requirements for Banks

  • Board-Approved Policy: Banks must have a dedicated policy covering appraisal mechanisms, Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) benchmarks, internal exposure limits, and monitoring covenants
  • Leverage Cap: Aggregate bank exposure to a borrowing InvIT — including exposure to underlying Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs) and holding companies — must not exceed 49% of the InvIT's asset value
  • Security Requirements: All financing must be fully secured through: charge over immovable property, assignment of project cash flows, pledge of equity interests in SPVs, and escrow arrangements for ring-fencing cash flows
  • Repayment Structure: Bullet repayments and ballooning repayment structures are prohibited; repayments must be structured in alignment with projected project cash flows

Prohibited Uses of Funds

  • Banks cannot lend to InvITs to fund financially distressed SPVs
  • Lending to facilitate acquisition of equity in other entities is not permitted

📅 Effective Date: October 1, 2026, or earlier if a bank adopts the directions in entirety. Existing non-conforming loans may run to maturity but cannot be renewed or enhanced.

2. Commercial Banks — REIT Risk Weight Treatment & Concentration Cap: A Three-Part Regulatory Package

RBI has issued three interlocking circulars establishing a coherent framework for bank exposures to Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs). These must be read together:

Part A — Risk Weight Treatment (8th Amendment Directions, 2026)

New paragraphs 61A and 61B have been inserted into the Capital Adequacy Directions, specifying the following risk-weight treatment for REIT exposures:

Exposure Type Risk Weight
Domestic REIT exposures (treated as Commercial Real Estate) 100%
REIT exposures qualifying as capital market exposure (Para 95A, Concentration Risk Directions) 125%
Overseas branches of Indian banks lending to REITs 150%

Part B — Concentration Risk Cap (3rd Amendment Directions, 2026)

Old paragraph 94 (general real estate sector exposure limit) has been deleted and replaced with new paragraphs 94A and sub-provisions:

  • Banks must establish Board-approved internal limits for aggregate real estate sector exposure with sub-limits across sub-categories
  • Aggregate exposure to REITs specifically is capped at 10% of the bank's eligible capital base
  • A corresponding sub-limit of 10% of eligible capital base applies to REIT exposures under the Credit Facilities Directions (RBI/2026-27/110)

📅 Effective Date for both: October 1, 2026, or earlier if the Credit Facilities Third Amendment is adopted in entirety by the bank.

3. RRB CRR & SLR — FCNR(B) Fresh Dollar Deposits Exempted

Following the RBI Governor's Statement of June 5, 2026 announcing a USD–Rupee swap facility, RBI has amended the Regional Rural Bank (RRB) CRR/SLR Directions to incentivise fresh FCNR(B) deposit mobilisation.

Key Exemption Details

  • Eligible deposits: Fresh FCNR(B) deposits of minimum 3-year and maximum 5-year tenor mobilised between June 8, 2026 and September 30, 2026 (including renewals at maturity)
  • CRR Exemption: Applies from the reporting fortnight beginning July 1, 2026 (based on NDTL as on June 15, 2026), continuing for subsequent fortnights as long as the deposits remain on books
  • SLR Exemption: Applies to original deposit amounts throughout their full tenure
  • Annex A Form A formats have also been correspondingly amended

The measure is designed to attract foreign currency inflows and strengthen India's foreign exchange reserve position.

4. RBI Cancels Certificates of Registration of 135 NBFCs

In a significant regulatory clean-up action, RBI — exercising powers under Section 45-IA(6) of the RBI Act, 1934 — has cancelled the Certificates of Registration (CoR) of 135 Non-Banking Financial Companies.

Entities whose registrations are cancelled are prohibited from carrying on the business of a non-banking financial institution. Stakeholders — including depositors, lenders, and contractual counterparties — of affected NBFCs must take immediate steps to assess the implications for existing obligations, deposits, and contracts.

⚠️ Action Required: If your NBFC or a counterparty NBFC is among the 135 entities, seek immediate legal and regulatory advice. Syns Associates can assist with classification of obligations and next steps.

Ministry of Corporate Affairs & IBBI

5. Companies Compliance Facilitation Scheme 2026 — July 15 Deadline Alert

The Companies Compliance Facilitation Scheme, 2026 (CCFS-2026), operative from April 15 to July 15, 2026, is in its final month. This is a one-time opportunity for companies with pending annual filings to regularise their compliance status with significantly reduced additional fees.

What Does the Scheme Cover?

  • Pending annual e-filings: MGT-7, MGT-7A, AOC-4, AOC-4 CFS, AOC-4 XBRL, and NBFC-specific forms
  • Inactive or defunct companies may opt for dormancy status or voluntary strike-off at reduced fees
  • Waiver of significant additional fees that would otherwise apply under normal filing procedures

⏰ Critical Deadline: July 15, 2026. Post this date, Registrars of Companies (ROCs) are directed to initiate action against non-compliant companies. All companies with any pending annual filings must act immediately.

6. IBBI Fourth Amendment — IBC Amendment Act Implementation

The Insolvency and Bankruptcy Board of India (IBBI) notified the IBBI (IRPCP) Fourth Amendment Regulations, 2026 on June 9, 2026, implementing further provisions of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (Amendment) Act, 2026 (Presidential assent: April 7, 2026; partially operative from May 25, 2026).

Read together with the 3rd Amendment (June 4) and 2nd Amendment (May 20), the reforms now fully operationalised include:

Reform Key Change
14-Day Mandatory Admission NCLT must admit or reject CIRP applications within 14 days; delays require recorded reasons
Expanded Pre-Pack (PPIRP) Pre-packaged insolvency extended beyond MSMEs to eligible mid-market companies
CIIRP (New Process) New Creditor-Initiated Insolvency Resolution Process introduced
Guarantor Asset Transfer (S. 28-A) Guarantor's assets may be transferred as part of CIRP with CoC approval (66% vote threshold where guarantor is also under insolvency)
Civil Penalty Regime (S. 235A) Adjudicating authority empowered to impose penalties up to 3× the loss caused or unlawful gain
Registered Valuer Definition Statutory definition aligned with the Companies Act, 2013 framework
Securities & Exchange Board of India

7. Merchant Banker SBU, Net Worth & Liquid Net Worth — Compliance Timelines Extended

SEBI, on June 11, 2026, extended multiple compliance deadlines for merchant bankers under the SEBI (Merchant Bankers) Regulations, 2025 (notified December 5, 2025). The extension follows representations from market participants regarding system-building complexities.

Requirement Original Deadline Revised Deadline
SBU Transfer [Reg. 13A(2)] July 3, 2026 December 31, 2026
Clause 11.2.10 Compliance July 3, 2026 December 31, 2026
Phase I Net Worth [Reg. 7] January 2, 2027 March 31, 2027
Phase II Net Worth January 2, 2028 March 31, 2028
Category I / II Intimation to SEBI January 2, 2027 March 31, 2027

ℹ️ All other provisions of the January 2, 2026 implementation circular remain unchanged and must be complied with as originally scheduled. Deadlines have been aligned with financial year-end dates as requested by industry.

Insurance Regulatory & Development Authority of India

8. IRDAI — Senior Executive Remuneration Linked to Policyholder Outcomes

IRDAI issued revised directions on June 10, 2026 mandating that variable pay structures for CEOs and Key Managerial Personnel (KMP) of insurance companies must reflect policyholder-centric performance outcomes. The following metrics are now mandatory determinants of executive compensation:

  • Claims settlement ratios and average turnaround times
  • Grievance redressal rates and resolution quality scores
  • Policyholder satisfaction indices and complaint volume trends

The revision follows IRDAI's Annual Report data revealing that Unfair Business Practices (UFBP) grievances against life insurers rose to 22.14% of total grievances in FY 2024–25, up from 19.33% the previous year — signalling persistent mis-selling concerns that the regulator is now directly addressing through compensation structures.

9. IRDAI — Health Insurance Performance Scorecard Framework Launched

IRDAI has commenced rollout of a public-facing performance scorecard system for health insurers and empanelled hospitals, developed in collaboration with the Confederation of Indian Industry (CII).

Key Features

  • Insurer Scorecards: Track claim settlement speed, billing accuracy, and documentation compliance across insurers
  • Hospital Scorecards: Hospitals rated on billing practices, claims processing timelines, and documentation standards. Hospital tariff reimbursements will be linked to performance scores
  • Public Access: Scorecards will be publicly accessible to enable informed comparison before purchasing or renewing health insurance
  • Affordable Products: IRDAI is simultaneously advancing basic, no-frills health insurance products to broaden coverage across income segments
  • Preventive Health Shift: The framework signals a structural move away from a purely hospitalisation-based model towards a preventive and wellness-oriented approach

💡 Syns Associates Advisory: Insurance companies and empanelled hospitals should begin internal audits of their claims processing timelines, grievance data, and billing documentation standards to prepare for the scorecard rollout. Early readiness will directly impact reimbursement rates and policyholder trust.

Key Deadlines

  • July 15, 2026 CCFS-2026 closes — last date to file pending MCA annual returns at reduced fees
  • September 30, 2026 Last date to mobilise FCNR(B) deposits eligible for CRR/SLR exemption
  • October 1, 2026 RBI InvIT/REIT lending framework effective; REIT risk weights apply
  • December 31, 2026 SEBI merchant banker SBU transfer & Clause 11.2.10 deadline
  • March 31, 2027 SEBI merchant banker Phase I Net Worth & Category intimation deadline
