NBFC & RBI

RBI's Revised NBFC Scale-Based Regulation Framework: Key Changes & Compliance Obligations in 2025

November 2025 · 8 min read · SYNS Associates Research Team

Background: Why RBI Introduced Scale-Based Regulation

The Reserve Bank of India introduced the Scale-Based Regulation (SBR) framework for Non-Banking Financial Companies in October 2021, effective April 1, 2022. The rationale was straightforward — the NBFC sector had grown enormously in size and systemic importance, yet a one-size-fits-all regulatory approach was creating distortions. Larger, systemically important NBFCs were operating with significantly lighter regulation than warranted by their risk profile.

The SBR framework replaced the earlier classification based primarily on deposit-taking vs. non-deposit-taking with a more nuanced, risk-based layered approach. The November 2025 revision tightened several thresholds and timelines, and NBFCs must act swiftly to ensure compliance.

The Four-Layer Classification

Under the SBR framework, all NBFCs are classified into four regulatory layers:

Layer Who Falls Here Regulatory Intensity
Base Layer (NBFC-BL) Non-deposit taking NBFCs with asset size below ₹1,000 crore; NBFC-P2P, NBFC-AA, Non-Operative Financial Holding Companies Lightest — existing regulations largely apply
Middle Layer (NBFC-ML) All deposit-taking NBFCs; non-deposit NBFCs with assets ≥ ₹1,000 crore; HFCs; IFCs; IDFs; CICs; SPDs Enhanced — stricter NPA, governance, and reporting norms
Upper Layer (NBFC-UL) Top 10 eligible NBFCs identified by RBI based on systemic risk scoring; any NBFC meeting quantitative thresholds Stringent — near-bank level regulations
Top Layer (NBFC-TL) NBFCs identified by RBI as posing systemic risk equivalent to banks; currently reserved/empty Bank-equivalent — full banking-level regulations would apply

Key Compliance Requirements by Layer

Net Owned Fund (NOF) Requirements

RBI has progressively raised minimum NOF requirements across layers. All new NBFCs seeking registration must comply with updated minimums. Existing NBFCs that fall short after the November 2025 revision must submit a board-approved capital roadmap to RBI within 90 days.

NPA Recognition Norms

  • Base Layer: 180-day NPA recognition norm (relaxed standard)
  • Middle Layer & above: 90-day NPA norm — fully aligned with bank-level recognition standards. The transition period granted in the original 2021 framework has now expired.

Capital Adequacy (CRAR)

  • Base Layer: Minimum CRAR of 15%
  • Middle Layer: Minimum CRAR of 15% with Tier I capital of at least 10%
  • Upper Layer: Additional Tier 1 (AT1) capital instruments permitted; CRAR minimum 15% with stricter Tier I floor

Governance Requirements

The SBR framework introduced significantly enhanced governance mandates, particularly for Middle and Upper Layer NBFCs:

  • Board Composition: ML and UL NBFCs must have a minimum number of Independent Directors (at least one-third of the Board)
  • Risk Management Committee: Mandatory at Board level for Upper Layer NBFCs; advisory for Middle Layer
  • Audit Committee: Must be chaired by an Independent Director in ML and UL
  • Chief Risk Officer (CRO): Mandatory appointment for Upper Layer NBFCs; CRO cannot be removed without RBI intimation
  • CEO/MD Remuneration: UL NBFCs must obtain RBI approval for CEO/MD/WTD compensation

Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)

The LCR requirement mandates that NBFCs maintain sufficient High Quality Liquid Assets (HQLA) to cover net cash outflows over a 30-day stress period. The November 2025 revision tightened LCR applicability:

  • Middle Layer NBFCs with asset size of ₹5,000 crore and above: LCR compliance mandatory at 100%
  • Upper Layer NBFCs: Full LCR compliance with daily reporting
  • Base Layer NBFCs remain exempt from formal LCR requirements

Large Exposure Framework for Upper Layer NBFCs

Upper Layer NBFCs must comply with the Large Exposure Framework (LEF), which limits exposure to a single counterparty or group of connected counterparties. Key limits:

  • Single counterparty: Maximum 25% of Tier 1 capital
  • Group of connected counterparties: Maximum 35% of Tier 1 capital
  • Intra-group transactions: Subject to Board-approved policy and enhanced disclosure

What Your NBFC Must Do Now

Regardless of your layer, the following steps are non-negotiable for 2025–26 compliance:

  1. Confirm your layer classification — Review your asset size, activity type, and RBI correspondence to confirm current layer
  2. Assess NOF adequacy — Verify your Net Owned Fund against the latest revised minimums
  3. NPA audit — Ensure your NPA recognition system reflects the correct day-count norm for your layer
  4. Board review — Verify Board composition meets Independent Director requirements
  5. LCR assessment — If in Middle or Upper layer above asset thresholds, establish LCR monitoring
  6. Regulatory returns — Confirm all NBS returns, ALM returns, and new SBR-mandated returns are being filed on schedule
  7. Update FPC and FMC policies — Fair Practices Code and Fair Merchant Code must be Board-approved and disclosed on website
