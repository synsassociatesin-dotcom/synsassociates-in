GST Amnesty Scheme 2024: Section 128A Waiver of Interest & Penalty — Everything Your Business Needs to Know

March 2025 · 6 min read · SYNS Associates Research Team

What Is Section 128A — The GST Amnesty Scheme?

The Finance (No. 2) Act, 2024 inserted a new Section 128A into the Central Goods and Services Tax (CGST) Act, 2017. This section provides a one-time amnesty opportunity — allowing businesses to settle pending GST demand notices for the financial years 2017–18, 2018–19, and 2019–20 by paying the tax dues alone, with a full waiver of interest and penalty.

This is one of the most significant relief measures extended to the taxpaying community since the rollout of GST. Thousands of businesses received demand notices under Sections 73 and 74 for the initial GST years, often involving genuine disputes or interpretation differences — and this scheme offers a clean exit.

⏰ Time-Sensitive: The scheme has a defined application window. Businesses must act before the deadline to avail the waiver. Contact SYNS Associates immediately to assess your eligibility and file your application.

Which Demands Qualify?

Section 128A specifically covers demand notices issued under:

  • Section 73 CGST Act — Demands for tax not paid, short paid, or erroneously refunded where fraud or willful misstatement is not alleged
  • Section 74 CGST Act — Demands where fraud or suppression is alleged (only the tax portion is waived of interest/penalty; tax must be paid in full)

The scheme covers demands for the following periods:

Financial Year Period Covered What is Waived
2017–18 July 2017 – March 2018 Interest & Penalty in full
2018–19 April 2018 – March 2019 Interest & Penalty in full
2019–20 April 2019 – March 2020 Interest & Penalty in full

Key Conditions to Avail the Waiver

The amnesty is not automatic — taxpayers must comply with specific conditions:

  1. Full payment of tax dues: The entire tax amount (as per the demand order) must be paid. Partial payment does not qualify for the waiver.
  2. Withdrawal of appeal: If the taxpayer has filed an appeal against the demand order (before any appellate authority, Tribunal, or Court), the appeal must be withdrawn or the taxpayer must give an undertaking to withdraw before the application is processed.
  3. Application filing: A formal application must be filed on the GST portal within the prescribed time window notified by the government.
  4. No refund of interest/penalty already paid: If you have already paid interest or penalties on these demands, there is no refund provision under Section 128A — the waiver is only prospective.

Demands Excluded from the Scheme

Not every GST demand qualifies. The following are specifically excluded:

  • Demands involving erroneous refund claims (Section 54 or 55)
  • Cases where criminal proceedings have been initiated
  • Demands already fully paid (including interest and penalty) — these are settled and no further action is needed
  • Demands for periods outside FY 2017–18 to 2019–20

How to Apply — Step-by-Step Process

  1. Identify eligible demand notices: Review all SCNs (Show Cause Notices) and demand orders received for FY 2017-18 to 2019-20 on the GST portal under 'View Notices and Orders'.
  2. Compute exact tax dues: Calculate the outstanding tax (excluding interest and penalty) from the demand order.
  3. Make payment: Pay the tax dues via the Electronic Liability Register (ELR) / PMT-06 on the GST portal. Keep payment challan records.
  4. Withdraw pending appeals (if any): File withdrawal application before the respective appellate authority or obtain a written undertaking. Ensure this step is completed before or simultaneously with the scheme application.
  5. File application on GST portal: Submit the prescribed application (Form GST SPL-02 as notified by CBIC) within the deadline, referencing the demand order number and payment details.
  6. Track and receive order: The proper officer will process and issue an order waiving the interest and penalty under Section 128A.

💡 Pro Tip: If you have multiple demand orders across different financial years, each order requires a separate application. SYNS Associates can handle the entire process — demand review, payment computation, appeal withdrawal, and application filing.

Strategic Considerations Before Opting In

The scheme sounds straightforward but has nuances that require careful analysis:

  • Assess merits of the demand: If your case has strong grounds for overturning the demand entirely at appeal or Tribunal, opting into the scheme (and withdrawing your appeal) may cost you more than paying the tax + forgoing the legal challenge would.
  • Interest vs. tax quantum: For old demands, the interest often exceeds the principal tax. In such cases, the scheme offers enormous savings — sometimes 50–100% of the total demand amount.
  • ITC availability: Check whether Input Tax Credit (ITC) can be utilised to pay the outstanding tax dues, or whether cash payment is required.
  • Multiple registrations: If your business has GSTIN registrations in multiple states and the same issue affects multiple registrations, each will need to be addressed separately.

Why This Scheme Matters for Indian Businesses

The early years of GST (2017–2020) were marked by genuine confusion — classification disputes, ITC mismatches, transition errors, and frequent rule changes. Many businesses received demand notices for issues that were interpretational rather than deliberate evasion. Section 128A acknowledges this reality and provides a resolution mechanism that serves both the government (faster tax recovery) and businesses (elimination of prolonged litigation costs).

For any business carrying open GST demands from these years, a thorough review of eligibility under Section 128A should be the top priority for the first half of 2025.

Quick Facts

  • Full waiver of interest & penalty
  • Only tax dues must be paid
  • Appeals must be withdrawn
  • Application on GST portal required
  • One-time, time-bound scheme
