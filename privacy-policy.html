Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 29, 2026  ·  Last Updated: June 29, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how SYNS Associates ("we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects personal information provided by visitors and clients. By using our website or engaging our services, you agree to the practices described herein.

1. Who We Are

SYNS Associates is a compliance and legal advisory firm providing services including company registration, GST compliance, income tax advisory, ROC and MCA filings, NBFC and RBI regulatory advisory, labour law compliance, trademark and IP registration, FEMA advisory, contract drafting, SEBI compliance, and startup advisory services to businesses across India.

For any privacy-related queries, you may contact us at:

  • Email: info@synsassociates.in
  • Phone: +91 70175 95859
  • Website: synsassociates.in

2. Information We Collect

We collect information in the following ways:

  • Information you provide directly: Name, email address, phone number, company name, nature of business, and any other details submitted via our contact form or during client onboarding.
  • Information collected automatically: Technical data such as IP address, browser type, pages visited, time spent on pages, and referring URL — collected via standard web server logs and analytics tools.
  • Information from professional engagement: Business documents, identification proofs, financial data, and other materials shared with us in the course of providing advisory or compliance services.

We do not collect sensitive personal data (such as passwords, payment card details, or biometric data) through our website.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your enquiries and provide the services you have requested
  • To send you relevant regulatory updates, compliance alerts, or firm communications (only where you have consented or have an existing professional relationship with us)
  • To fulfil our legal and regulatory obligations as a professional services firm
  • To improve our website, services, and client experience
  • To maintain accurate records as required by applicable law

We do not use your information for automated decision-making or profiling.

4. Legal Basis for Processing (DPDPA 2023)

We process your personal data in accordance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDPA) and other applicable Indian laws. Our lawful bases for processing are:

  • Consent: Where you have voluntarily submitted your information through our contact form or opted into communications.
  • Contract performance: Where processing is necessary to deliver services you have engaged us for.
  • Legitimate interests: For website analytics, internal record-keeping, and improving our services — where such interests do not override your fundamental rights.
  • Legal obligation: Where we are required to retain or share data by applicable law or regulatory authority.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share your information only in the following circumstances:

  • With government and regulatory authorities: As required for filings, registrations, and compliance work performed on your behalf (e.g., MCA, GST portal, Income Tax Department, RBI, SEBI).
  • With trusted service providers: Technology vendors, email service providers, or document management tools that assist in running our practice — bound by confidentiality obligations.
  • With your explicit consent: In any other case where you have authorised us in writing.
  • For legal compliance: Where disclosure is required under law, court order, or by a competent regulatory authority.

6. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data from unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These measures include secure communication channels, access controls, and confidentiality obligations on all our team members.

While we take all reasonable precautions, no method of data transmission over the internet is 100% secure. You share data with us at your own risk, and we recommend not transmitting sensitive financial or personal information via unsecured channels.

7. Data Retention

We retain personal data for as long as necessary to fulfil the purpose for which it was collected, or as required by law. Specifically:

  • Client engagement records are retained for a minimum of 8 years in accordance with professional standards and applicable statutory requirements.
  • Website enquiry data is retained for up to 24 months unless a professional relationship commences, in which case it forms part of the client record.
  • Marketing communications data is retained until you withdraw consent or request deletion.

8. Your Rights

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, and as a matter of professional practice, you have the following rights:

  • Right to access: Request a summary of personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure: Request deletion of your data, subject to our legal retention obligations.
  • Right to withdraw consent: Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting prior processing.
  • Right to grievance redressal: You may raise a complaint with us and, if unresolved, with the Data Protection Board of India once it is operationalised.

To exercise any of these rights, please write to us at info@synsassociates.in with subject line "Privacy Request". We will respond within 30 days.

9. Cookies

Our website may use basic cookies for analytics and session management. These cookies do not collect personally identifiable information. You may disable cookies through your browser settings; however, this may affect your experience on the site. We do not use third-party advertising cookies.

10. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites (such as government portals, regulatory authorities, or publications). We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of such external sites. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party websites you visit.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable law. The revised policy will be posted on this page with an updated effective date. Continued use of our website or services after such changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
