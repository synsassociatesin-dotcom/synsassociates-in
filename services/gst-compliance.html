From GST registration to monthly returns, annual reconciliation, e-invoicing, and notice management — end-to-end GST compliance so you never miss a deadline or lose eligible Input Tax Credit.

GST Compliance in India — The Stakes

Goods and Services Tax (GST), introduced under the CGST Act, 2017, is a destination-based multi-stage indirect tax that has unified India's fragmented tax structure. While GST simplified the tax landscape in theory, the compliance obligations it creates — multiple monthly and annual return filings, Input Tax Credit reconciliation, e-invoicing mandates, and regular amendments — demand expert and continuous attention.

Failure to file on time attracts a late fee of ₹50 per day per return (₹20 for nil returns), plus 18% per annum interest on outstanding tax. Mismatches between GSTR-2B and purchase registers trigger scrutiny notices. At SYNS Associates, our dedicated GST team manages the entire compliance lifecycle so your business remains clean on the GST portal year-round.

Our GST Services

  • GST Registration: New registration on the GST portal under CGST/SGST/IGST as regular taxpayer, composition dealer, or casual taxable person. Turnover threshold: ₹40 lakh for goods, ₹20 lakh for services (₹10 lakh in special category states).
  • GSTR-1 Filing: Monthly/quarterly outward supply return reporting all B2B and B2C sales, exports, debit/credit notes, and HSN-wise summary.
  • GSTR-3B Filing: Monthly summary return with ITC claim and net tax liability computation and payment. Due by 20th of the following month for regular taxpayers.
  • GSTR-9 & GSTR-9C (Annual Return): Consolidated annual return for FY 2025–26 due by 31 December 2026. GSTR-9C (reconciliation statement) is mandatory for taxpayers with aggregate annual turnover exceeding ₹5 crore.
  • GSTR-2B Reconciliation: Monthly reconciliation of purchase records against auto-generated GSTR-2B to maximise eligible ITC and identify non-compliant vendors.
  • E-Invoicing Compliance: IRN (Invoice Reference Number) generation on the IRP portal is mandatory for taxpayers with AATO ≥ ₹10 crore. We configure your billing system and ensure timely IRN generation within the 30-day reporting window.
  • E-Way Bill Management: Generation and management of e-way bills for intra-state and inter-state movement of goods valued above ₹50,000.
  • GST Notice & Assessment Response: Drafting and filing responses to scrutiny notices, demand orders, and audit queries from GST authorities.
  • GST Amendment & Cancellation: Amendments to registration details, addition/deletion of business verticals, and voluntary GST cancellation.
  • Composition Scheme Advisory: Eligibility assessment and migration to/from composition scheme (GSTR-4 filing).

Key Compliance Updates (FY 2025–26)

  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Mandatory for all GST portal users from 1 April 2025 onwards, regardless of turnover.
  • E-Invoicing Threshold: ₹10 crore AATO threshold remains in force; IRN must be reported to IRP within 30 days of invoice date. IRN generation is blocked beyond this window.
  • ITC Reversal Rules: ITC must be reversed for any invoice not appearing in GSTR-2B within the relevant return period. Rule 86B also restricts cash ledger use to 1% of tax liability for certain taxpayers.
  • GST Amnesty: The 53rd GST Council extended the amnesty scheme for reversal of demands under Section 128A for FY 2017–18 to 2019–20. Taxpayers who pay the principal demand in full by 31 March 2026 are waived of interest and penalties.

ITC Reconciliation is Critical: Always reconcile your GSTR-2B with your purchase register before filing GSTR-3B. Unreconciled differences can lead to ITC reversal demands with 18% interest and potential fraud allegations. Our team performs monthly reconciliation as part of the retainer engagement.

Our GST Compliance Process

  1. Data CollectionWe collect your sales and purchase data in any format — Excel, ERP export, Tally, or direct portal access — by the 5th of each month.
  2. Reconciliation & ITC OptimisationPurchase data is reconciled against GSTR-2B. Vendor non-filers are flagged. All eligible ITC is claimed; ineligible credits are identified and reversed.
  3. Return PreparationGSTR-1 and GSTR-3B are prepared, reviewed for accuracy, and shared with you for approval before filing.
  4. Filing & PaymentReturns are filed on the GST portal. Tax payable is computed and the payment challan (PMT-06) is generated for your approval and transfer.
  5. Acknowledgement & Filing StatusFiled ARNs (Acknowledgement Reference Numbers) are shared immediately post-filing. Any portal errors are resolved within 24 hours.
