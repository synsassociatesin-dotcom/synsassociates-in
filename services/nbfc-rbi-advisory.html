NBFC Registration & RBI Advisory

End-to-end NBFC registration under the revised RBI framework — Certificate of Registration, SBR classification, PRAVAAH portal applications, ongoing RBI returns, KYC/AML policy drafting, and regulatory advisory for NBFCs at every stage of growth.

The New NBFC Regulatory Landscape (2025–26)

On 28 November 2025, the Reserve Bank of India issued comprehensive new consolidated directions titled the RBI (Non-Banking Financial Companies Registration, Exemptions and Framework for Scale Based Regulation) Directions, 2025, replacing the earlier scattered circulars and Master Directions. These directions, effective 1 July 2026, fundamentally reorganise how NBFCs are classified, registered, and regulated in India.

The new framework introduces three distinct categories — Type I NBFC, Type II NBFC, and Unregistered Type I NBFC — replacing the earlier deposit-taking/non-deposit-taking distinction. The PRAVAAH portal (Platform for Regulatory Application, VAlidation, and Authorisation) is now the single window for all new NBFC registration applications, significantly streamlining the process for well-prepared applicants. Companies with pending or new applications must align with this framework immediately.

New NBFC Classification Framework

CategoryKey CriteriaRegistration Required?
Type II NBFCAvails public funds and/or has customer interfaceYes — mandatory CoR from RBI
Type I NBFCNo public funds, no customer interface, assets ≥ ₹1,000 croreYes — CoR as Type I NBFC
Unregistered Type I NBFCNo public funds, no customer interface, assets < ₹1,000 croreNo — exempt from Sections 45-IA & 45-IC of RBI Act

Note: Existing CoR holders who qualify as Unregistered Type I NBFCs may apply for deregistration via PRAVAAH by 31 December 2026. Annual board resolution and auditor disclosure are mandatory for continued exempt status.

Our NBFC & RBI Advisory Services

  • NBFC Registration (CoR Application): Complete preparation and filing of the Certificate of Registration application on PRAVAAH portal — eligibility assessment, net-owned fund verification (minimum ₹10 crore for most categories), business plan, board composition review, and documentation.
  • SBR Classification Advisory: Assessment of which SBR layer (Base Layer, Middle Layer, Upper Layer, Top Layer) your NBFC falls under and the corresponding regulatory obligations — capital adequacy, leverage ratio, liquidity, governance, and disclosure requirements at each layer.
  • Ongoing RBI Return Filing: Monthly, quarterly, and annual XBRL-based returns to the RBI — including NBS-1 to NBS-7, DNBS forms, CRILC reporting, and liquidity coverage ratio (LCR) returns.
  • KYC & AML Policy Drafting: Customer Due Diligence (CDD) procedures, Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) for high-risk customers, Suspicious Transaction Reporting (STR) framework, and FIU-IND compliance in accordance with PML Act, 2002 and RBI Master Direction on KYC.
  • Fair Practices Code (FPC): Drafting and board adoption of Fair Practices Code as required under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 and RBI directions.
  • IS Audit & Cyber Security Framework: Assistance with Information Security Audit requirements for NBFCs in Middle and Upper Layer per RBI's IT framework.
  • Internal Audit & Compliance Function Setup: Setting up the compliance function, Chief Compliance Officer (CCO) role documentation, and internal audit charter as mandated for upper-layer NBFCs.
  • RBI Inspection Response: Preparing responses and compliance reports for RBI's Annual Financial Inspection (AFI) and DNBS inspections.
  • Compounding Applications: Filing compounding applications for FEMA and RBI Act contraventions under the applicable guidelines.
  • NBFC-Account Aggregator, P2P, HFC, MFI Advisory: Specialised advisory for specific NBFC categories including Account Aggregators, Peer-to-Peer lending platforms, Housing Finance Companies (post-NHB transfer), and NBFC-MFIs.

NBFC Registration Process

  1. Pre-Application AssessmentWe assess eligibility — net-owned funds (minimum ₹10 crore), promoter background, proposed NBFC type, and principal business test compliance. We advise on the correct category before applying.
  2. Company Incorporation & Capital ArrangementIf not already incorporated, we register the NBFC company under Companies Act, 2013. Minimum paid-up capital is arranged and bank certificate obtained confirming funds in current account.
  3. Document PreparationPreparation of detailed business plan (3 years), board members' CVs, promoter net worth certificates, CIBIL reports, model FPC, KYC/AML policy, MoA objects verification, and all RBI-specified annexures.
  4. PRAVAAH Portal Application FilingComplete application filed on the RBI's PRAVAAH portal. Two-track processing — Track 1 for routine applications, Track 2 for complex cases requiring additional scrutiny.
  5. RBI Query ResolutionWe prepare detailed responses to any clarifications or additional document requests from the RBI's Department of Regulation within specified timelines.
  6. CoR Issuance & Post-Registration ComplianceOnce CoR is issued, we set up the entire compliance calendar for ongoing RBI returns, board governance requirements, and SBR obligations.

Important — April 2026 Deregulation Window: Existing NBFCs that do not avail public funds and have no customer interface with assets below ₹1,000 crore may apply for deregistration from the RBI by 31 December 2026 via PRAVAAH. This eliminates the cost and burden of RBI compliance for eligible companies. SYNS Associates can assess your eligibility and handle the deregistration application.
