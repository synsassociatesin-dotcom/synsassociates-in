Trademark Registration & IP Services

Protect your brand, inventions, and creative works — trademark search and registration, copyright, design, and patent filing assistance, plus ongoing IP portfolio management for Indian and global markets.

Why Intellectual Property Protection Cannot Wait

Your brand name, logo, product design, software, creative content, and inventions are among the most valuable assets your business owns — but only if legally protected. India follows a "first to file" principle for trademarks, which means any third party can register your brand name before you do if you delay. Once registered, a trademark grants its owner the exclusive right to use the mark in the registered class for an initial 10-year period, renewable indefinitely every 10 years.

India recorded over 95% of trademark applications filed online in 2025, and the IP India portal has significantly improved processing timelines for well-prepared applications. SYNS Associates handles trademark applications, objection responses, copyright registration, and IP enforcement advisory across all 45 NICE Classification classes.

Our Intellectual Property Services

  • Trademark Search: Comprehensive search on the IP India public database and common law search to identify conflicting marks, phonetic similarities, and potential hurdles before filing — reducing the risk of rejection or opposition.
  • Trademark Registration (Form TM-A): Preparation and filing of trademark applications for word marks, logos, device marks, colour marks, and three-dimensional marks across any of the 45 NICE classification classes via the IP India online portal.
  • Government Filing Fee (FY 2025–26): ₹4,500 per class for individuals, startups, and MSMEs; ₹9,000 per class for companies, LLPs, and other entities (online filing; physical filing is ₹500 higher per class).
  • Trademark Objection Response: Drafting detailed responses to Examination Reports issued by the Trade Mark Registry within 30 days — addressing absolute and relative grounds of refusal with supporting evidence.
  • Trademark Opposition Proceedings: Filing notices of opposition against conflicting marks (Form TM-O) and representing clients in opposition hearings before the Trade Marks Registry.
  • Trademark Renewal (Form TM-R): Renewal of registered trademarks at the end of every 10-year period. We track expiry dates and file renewals proactively.
  • Trademark Assignment & Licensing: Preparation of trademark assignment deeds and licence agreements; recording of assignments with the Trade Marks Registry.
  • Copyright Registration: Registration of original literary, dramatic, musical, artistic, cinematographic, and sound recording works with the Copyright Office, Ministry of Education (Form XIV).
  • Design Registration: Registration of original industrial designs (shape, configuration, pattern, or ornamentation) under the Designs Act, 2000 — protection for 10 years, extendable by 5 years.
  • Patent Filing Assistance: Coordination with patent agents for provisional and complete patent specification filing under the Patents Act, 1970. We handle pre-filing strategy, inventor documentation, and liaison with the Indian Patent Office.
  • Madrid Protocol (International TM): Filing international trademark applications via the WIPO Madrid System for protection in 130+ member countries using a single application.
  • IP Watch & Monitoring: Monthly monitoring of new trademark filings for conflicts with your registered marks — enabling timely opposition before conflicting marks become registered.

Trademark Registration Process

  1. Mark Assessment & Class IdentificationWe review the proposed mark for registrability — distinctiveness, existing conflicts — and identify the most appropriate NICE classes for your business.
  2. Comprehensive TM SearchFull search on IP India portal including identical, similar, and phonetically alike marks in the target classes. Written search report shared with recommendations.
  3. Application Preparation & FilingForm TM-A filed online on the IP India portal with a specimen of use or proposed use, applicant details, and class specifications. Government fee paid at filing.
  4. Examination & Objection Response (if any)Within 3–6 months, the Examiner issues a report. If objections are raised, we prepare a detailed response within the 30-day window. Hearings before the Hearing Officer are attended by our IP advocates.
  5. Publication in Trade Marks JournalIf accepted, the mark is published in the Trade Marks Journal for 4 months — allowing third parties to file oppositions. We monitor for opposing parties during this period.
  6. Registration CertificateIf no opposition is filed (or if opposition proceedings are resolved in your favour), the Trade Marks Registry issues the Registration Certificate. Total timeline: typically 18–24 months from filing date.

Use ™ Immediately, ® Only After Registration: Once you file a trademark application, you are entitled to use the ™ symbol next to your mark. You may only use the ® symbol after the Certificate of Registration is issued. Misuse of ® before registration can attract legal liability under the Trade Marks Act, 1999.
