Startup Services & Funding Compliance

From DPIIT recognition and tax exemptions to funding documentation, ESOP structuring, and scaling compliance — SYNS Associates is the legal and regulatory partner for India's founder-led businesses at every stage of growth.

Building a Startup on a Compliant Foundation

India's startup ecosystem is among the most vibrant in the world — over 1.5 lakh DPIIT-recognised startups as of 2026, with new policy frameworks expanding eligibility criteria and tax benefits. However, the complexity of startup compliance is often underestimated. Mis-structured cap tables, incorrectly executed ESOP grants, missed FDI reporting after angel rounds, or failure to obtain DPIIT recognition can have cascading consequences during due diligence for subsequent funding rounds or at the time of an exit.

SYNS Associates serves as a long-term legal and compliance partner to founders — from incorporation and DPIIT recognition through Series A, B, and beyond. We understand both the regulatory requirements and the commercial realities of early-stage businesses, and we price our startup services to be founder-accessible without compromising on quality or depth.

Our Startup Services

  • Startup Incorporation Advisory: Selection of the optimal entity structure for a startup planning VC funding — Private Limited Company (recommended for equity investment), LLP (for service businesses without equity funding plans), or OPC (for solo founders). Key consideration: only a Private Limited Company can issue ESOP or raise institutional equity.
  • DPIIT Startup India Recognition (2026 Framework): Preparation and filing of the DPIIT Startup India recognition application on the startupindia.gov.in portal. The revised February 2026 Gazette Notification expanded eligibility — turnover ceiling raised to ₹300 crore for deep tech startups, period of existence extended to 20 years for deep tech, and Multi-State Cooperative Societies now included. Standard recognition turnaround: 7–15 days.
  • Section 80-IAC Tax Exemption: Application to the Inter-Ministerial Board (IMB) for the 3-year income tax holiday under Section 80-IAC of the Income Tax Act for DPIIT-recognised startups incorporated after 1 April 2016. Only IMB-certified startups are eligible for the ESOP tax deferral benefit.
  • Labour Law Self-Certification: DPIIT-recognised startups are eligible for self-certification under 9 labour laws and 3 environmental laws for 3 years from recognition — avoiding government inspections under these laws. We prepare the self-certification declarations and compliance documentation.
  • ESOP Structuring & Compliance: Drafting of the Employee Stock Option Plan (ESOP) scheme document, board and shareholder resolutions (MGT-14 filing), individual grant letters, vesting schedules, and exercise documentation. Under the new Income Tax Act, 2025, the ESOP tax deferral window is 60 months from the end of the tax year of allotment for shares allotted on/after 1 April 2026 (for IMB-certified startups).
  • Seed / Angel Round Compliance: Term sheet review, Subscription and Shareholders' Agreement (SSA/SHA) drafting, CCPS/CCD instrument documentation, pre-money valuation review, and post-closing FEMA reporting (FC-GPR within 30 days of share allotment).
  • Angel Tax Compliance (Section 56(2)(viib)): Preparation of valuation reports by registered merchant bankers for angel investment rounds, 56(2)(viib) exemption applicability analysis, and Form 3CN filing for DPIIT-recognised startups (exempt from angel tax under the 2023 amendment). Note: The Finance Act 2024 expanded angel tax exemption for all recognised startups effective 1 April 2024.
  • Cap Table Management: Digital cap table setup, modelling of conversion scenarios (CCPS to equity, CCD conversion), dilution analysis for future rounds, and ESOP pool sizing recommendations.
  • Convertible Instrument Documentation: Drafting of Compulsorily Convertible Preference Shares (CCPS), Compulsorily Convertible Debentures (CCD), and SAFE Note structures adapted for Indian regulatory compliance.
  • IP Assignment & Protection: Assignment of founder-created IP to the company, IP audit prior to funding, trademark registration for the startup brand, and technology agreement drafting for SaaS and deep tech startups.
  • Startup Data Privacy Compliance: Advisory on the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDPA) obligations for startups collecting personal data of Indian users — data principal consent, Data Protection Officer (DPO) requirement, data processing agreements, and breach notification obligations.

Angel Tax — Fully Abolished for DPIIT-Recognised Startups: The Finance Act 2024 abolished angel tax (Section 56(2)(viib)) for all investments in DPIIT-recognised startups received from resident investors, effective from 1 April 2024. Combined with the earlier exemption for foreign investors, this means DPIIT-recognised startups are now completely free from angel tax liability. Obtaining DPIIT recognition before your next funding round is therefore a high-priority task.

DPIIT Recognition Process

  1. Eligibility AssessmentWe verify that your entity meets the incorporation date, turnover, entity type, and innovation criteria under the February 2026 Gazette Notification before preparing the application.
  2. Document PreparationCoI, PAN, audited financials (if available), brief description of the innovative product/service, and video pitch (optional). Application is largely automated on the Startup India portal.
  3. Portal Application FilingApplication filed on startupindia.gov.in by our team. Government processing typically completes in 7–15 working days. We track the application status and respond to any queries from DPIIT.
  4. Recognition CertificateDPIIT Recognition Certificate issued digitally. We then initiate ESOP scheme registration, self-certification under labour and environmental laws, and IMB application for Section 80-IAC exemption (if eligible).
