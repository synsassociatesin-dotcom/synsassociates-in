SEBI Compliance — The Stakes for Listed Entities

Listing on BSE or NSE brings the full weight of SEBI's regulatory oversight — covering continuous disclosures, periodic financial reporting, corporate governance standards, insider trading restrictions, and related party transaction approvals. The Securities and Exchange Board of India (LODR) Regulations, 2015 — last amended on 22 January 2026 — and the SEBI Master Circular (last updated 30 January 2026) consolidated all prior circulars into a single framework governing listed entities.

Non-compliance attracts significant penalties: up to ₹25 crore or three times the unlawful gain under Section 15 of the SEBI Act, 1992, trading window violations under the PIT Regulations can trigger criminal prosecution, and failure to make timely Regulation 30 disclosures can result in surveillance actions. SYNS Associates provides retainer-based SEBI compliance management for listed companies and pre-IPO advisory for companies planning a public listing.

Our SEBI Compliance Services

  • LODR Compliance Calendar Management: End-to-end management of all SEBI LODR periodic compliance obligations — quarterly, half-yearly, and annual — ensuring timely submissions to BSE/NSE stock exchange portals.
  • Regulation 30 (Material Event Disclosures): Identification of material events and price-sensitive information (MPSI) requiring immediate disclosure to stock exchanges. Drafting and filing disclosures within 24 hours (or the prescribed timeline) for events including board decisions, financial results, mergers, legal proceedings, regulatory orders, and key management changes.
  • Financial Results Filing: Preparation and filing of quarterly (within 45 days of quarter-end) and annual audited financial results (within 60 days of financial year-end) with stock exchanges in the prescribed format.
  • PIT Compliance — Insider Trading Framework: Drafting and implementation of the Code of Conduct for Prevention of Insider Trading as required under SEBI (Prohibition of Insider Trading) Regulations, 2015. Trading window management, designated persons register, pre-clearance process for trades above threshold, and structured digital database for UPSI (unpublished price-sensitive information).
  • Compliance Officer Support: Every listed entity must appoint a qualified Company Secretary as Compliance Officer responsible for LODR compliance, exchange coordination, investor grievances, and board meeting procedures. We provide compliance officer support services where the listed entity lacks dedicated in-house capacity.
  • SAST (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers): Advisory on SEBI Takeover Code Regulations, 2011 — triggering events (creeping acquisition beyond 25% / 5% per year), public announcement obligations, open offer structuring, and exemption applications.
  • Related Party Transaction (RPT) Compliance: Materiality policy drafting, audit committee approval workflow, shareholder approval where required (transactions exceeding 10% of annual consolidated turnover), and quarterly RPT disclosures under Regulation 23.
  • Corporate Governance Reports: Preparation of the Corporate Governance Report (within 21 days of each quarter end), Business Responsibility & Sustainability Report (BRSR — mandatory for Top 1,000 listed companies by market cap), and Annual Report disclosures.
  • Shareholder Meeting Compliance: AGM notice drafting, postal ballot (e-voting) process management, scrutiniser appointment, proxy advisory analysis, and post-meeting disclosure filings.
  • SEBI Adjudication & Notice Response: Representation before SEBI Adjudicating Officers (AO) in penalty proceedings, filing of replies and settlements under SEBI's settlement scheme, and appellate proceedings before the Securities Appellate Tribunal (SAT).
  • Pre-IPO SEBI Advisory: Guidance on SEBI ICDR (Issue of Capital and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2018 for companies planning an Initial Public Offering — eligibility, lock-in periods, promoter minimum contribution, DRHP preparation timeline, and financial disclosures.

SEBI Master Circular — January 2026: SEBI issued a revised Master Circular for compliance with LODR Regulations on 30 January 2026, consolidating all circulars up to 30 December 2025. Key changes include updated timelines for material event disclosures under Regulation 30, a revised materiality policy framework, enhanced related party transaction disclosure requirements, and strengthened board independence norms. All listed companies must review their compliance frameworks against the January 2026 Master Circular immediately.
