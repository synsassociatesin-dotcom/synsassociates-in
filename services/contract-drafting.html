Contract Drafting & Legal Documentation

Precisely drafted commercial contracts that protect your business interests — from founder agreements and shareholder documents to technology contracts, employment agreements, and complex transaction documentation, all governed by Indian law.

Why Contract Quality Defines Business Risk

A contract is only as strong as its drafting. Ambiguous terms, missing representations and warranties, improperly defined dispute resolution clauses, or absence of limitation of liability provisions can expose your business to multi-crore disputes that could have been avoided with precise legal drafting. Under the Indian Contract Act, 1872, and the Specific Relief Act, 1963, courts interpret contracts strictly — gaps in drafting are resolved against the party who could have specified the term more clearly.

At SYNS Associates, every contract is drafted by qualified advocates with sector-specific experience. We do not use generic templates — each document is customised to reflect your commercial intent, risk appetite, and regulatory obligations. We also review and redline contracts presented by counter-parties, identifying clauses that are commercially unfavourable or legally unenforceable under Indian law before you sign.

Contracts We Draft

  • Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) / Confidentiality Agreement: Unilateral and mutual NDAs for business discussions, vendor engagements, employment, and technology sharing. Includes definition of confidential information, exclusions, permitted disclosures, duration, and injunctive relief clauses.
  • Founders' Agreement: Roles and responsibilities, equity split, vesting schedules, IP assignment to company, exit provisions, non-compete obligations, and dispute resolution between co-founders — critical before incorporating or raising any funding.
  • Shareholder Agreement (SHA): Rights of investors (CCPS, CCD, equity), board governance, reserved matters, tag-along and drag-along rights, right of first refusal (ROFR), anti-dilution protection, liquidation preference, and exit mechanisms. Compliant with Companies Act, 2013 and SEBI LODR (for listed entities).
  • Subscription and Share Purchase Agreement (SSA/SPA): Investment documentation for equity and convertible instrument transactions — representations and warranties, conditions precedent, closing conditions, indemnities, and post-closing obligations.
  • Employment Contract / Appointment Letter: Comprehensive employment agreements covering CTC structure, confidentiality, IP assignment, non-solicitation, non-compete (time and geography bound), termination provisions, and grievance redressal procedures. Aligned with new Labour Code requirements.
  • Vendor / Supplier Agreement: Supply terms, pricing, delivery schedules, acceptance criteria, warranty provisions, liability caps, indemnity, termination rights, and governing law. Industry-specific versions for manufacturing, technology, and services sectors.
  • Service Level Agreement (SLA): Performance standards, uptime commitments, measurement methodology, remedies for breach, credits, escalation procedures, and limitation of liability provisions for service providers and enterprise clients.
  • Technology / SaaS Agreement: Software licence (perpetual and subscription), SaaS terms of service, data processing agreement (DPA) for GDPR and PDPB compliance, API agreements, and software escrow arrangements.
  • Joint Venture Agreement (JVA): Governance structure, contribution of capital and resources, profit-sharing mechanism, operational control, deadlock provisions, transfer restrictions, and exit buyout arrangements.
  • Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) / Term Sheet: Non-binding commercial frameworks for business arrangements, with careful carve-outs for binding provisions (exclusivity, confidentiality, governing law).
  • Lease and Licence Agreements: Commercial property leases, leave and licence agreements, co-working space licences, and rent deeds under the Transfer of Property Act, 1882 and relevant state Rent Control laws.
  • LLP Agreement / Partnership Deed: Contribution, profit/loss sharing, management rights, admission and retirement of partners, dissolution, and indemnity provisions under the LLP Act, 2008 or Indian Partnership Act, 1932.

Contract Review & Redlining

We review contracts received from counter-parties — investors, clients, vendors, employers — and provide a structured redline with our comments on:

  • Clauses that create unlimited or asymmetric liability for your entity
  • Representations and warranties that are inaccurate or impossible to satisfy
  • Dispute resolution clauses that place you in an unfavourable jurisdiction or arbitration seat
  • IP ownership terms that inadvertently transfer ownership of your core technology or inventions
  • Exit restrictions that could trap your equity without fair valuation rights
  • Governing law and jurisdiction clauses that may be unenforceable under Indian law

Arbitration in India — Important Update: The Arbitration and Conciliation (Amendment) Act, 2021 introduced significant changes — including a 60-day time limit for the filing of statements of claim/defence, a revised framework for emergency arbitrators, and more effective enforcement of foreign awards. Ensure your arbitration clause specifies a clear seat of arbitration, institutional rules (DIAC, ICADR, SIAC, or ICC), number of arbitrators, and language. We draft enforceable arbitration clauses as standard in all commercial contracts.

Our Contract Drafting Process

  • Briefing Call: We understand the commercial context, counter-party dynamics, negotiation history, and specific risk concerns before drafting begins.
  • First Draft (within 3–5 business days): Delivered in Microsoft Word (track changes enabled) with explanatory comments on key commercial and legal choices.
  • Client Review & Revision Rounds: Typically 2 revision rounds included in the fixed fee. Additional rounds billed at standard hourly rates.
  • Negotiation Support: We attend or advise during negotiations with the counter-party — either in person, via call, or by reviewing the counter-party's redline and recommending positions.
  • Execution Support: Guidance on stamping (stamp duty under respective State Stamp Acts), notarisation (where required), and digital execution via DocuSign or in-person.
