Direct Tax

Income Tax & TDS Compliance

Accurate ITR filing, timely TDS deduction and deposit, advance tax computation, and expert representation before Income Tax authorities — all under one roof.

Direct Tax Compliance Is Non-Negotiable

The Income Tax Act, 1961 (and the new Income Tax Act, 2025, effective 1 April 2026) imposes a comprehensive set of obligations on individuals, companies, LLPs, trusts, and other entities operating in India. Beyond filing an annual return, businesses must manage Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) on every qualifying payment, compute and deposit advance tax in four quarterly instalments, and respond promptly to notices from the Income Tax Department.

Non-compliance attracts penal interest under Sections 234A, 234B, and 234C, prosecution risk under Section 276CC, and disallowance of legitimate business expenditure under Section 40(a). SYNS Associates assigns a dedicated tax manager to each client, ensuring zero missed deadlines and proactive tax optimisation — not just compliance after the fact.

Our Income Tax & TDS Services

  • Income Tax Return (ITR) Filing: All ITR forms — ITR-1 through ITR-7 — for individuals, HUFs, firms, LLPs, companies (including Section 8), trusts, and AOPs. Includes preparation, review, and e-filing with digital signature where applicable.
  • Tax Audit (Form 3CA/3CB & 3CD): Mandatory for companies and individuals with turnover exceeding ₹1 crore (₹10 crore for digital transactions under Section 44AB). Conducted by our panel of statutory auditors.
  • Transfer Pricing (Form 3CEB): Annual transfer pricing documentation and accountant's report for international and specified domestic transactions as required under Sections 92–92F.
  • Advance Tax Computation: Calculation of quarterly advance tax liability (June 15 / September 15 / December 15 / March 15) to avoid interest under Section 234C.
  • TDS Deduction & Deposit Management: End-to-end TDS compliance — identifying deductible payments, applying correct rates (or lower-deduction certificates), and depositing TDS to the government before the 7th of the following month (30 April for March).
  • TDS Return Filing: Quarterly filing of Form 24Q (salary TDS), 26Q (non-salary payments to residents), 27Q (payments to non-residents), and 27EQ (TCS collection at source).
  • Form 15CA / 15CB: Chartered Accountant's certificate and online filing for remittances to non-residents under Section 195 as required by Rule 37BB.
  • Form 26AS & AIS Reconciliation: Reconciliation of Annual Information Statement (AIS) and TIS data with books of accounts to identify mismatches before IT filing.
  • Lower TDS Deduction Certificates: Applications under Section 197 for lower or nil TDS deduction certificates on behalf of eligible recipients.
  • Income Tax Notice & Assessment Response: Preparation and filing of responses to notices under Sections 143(1), 143(2) (scrutiny), 148 (reassessment), 263 (revision), and 271 (penalty proceedings).
  • Appeals: Representation before the Commissioner of Income Tax (Appeals) — CIT(A) and the Income Tax Appellate Tribunal (ITAT).

New Income Tax Act, 2025: India's comprehensive new Income Tax Act came into force from 1 April 2026 (AY 2026–27 onwards), consolidating and simplifying the 1961 Act. Key changes include a revised default tax regime for individuals (the new regime now applies by default unless the taxpayer opts out), updated slab rates, and modified ESOP deferral windows (60 months from end of relevant tax year for allotments on or after 1 April 2026). Our team is fully updated on the new Act.
