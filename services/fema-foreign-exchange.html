FEMA Compliance — High Stakes, Tight Timelines

The Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999 (FEMA) governs all cross-border transactions involving foreign exchange — including FDI received by Indian companies, overseas investments by Indian entities, international remittances, and external commercial borrowings. FEMA violations attract compounding penalties, enforcement proceedings by the Enforcement Directorate (ED), and reputational risk that can derail funding rounds, acquisitions, and business partnerships.

Over 90% of sectors now permit 100% FDI under the automatic route, but this does not eliminate reporting obligations. Every inflow and outflow of foreign exchange must be reported to the RBI within strict timelines through the FIRMS (Foreign Investment Reporting and Management System) portal via the company's Authorised Dealer (AD) bank. SYNS Associates provides end-to-end FEMA advisory and reporting for companies receiving foreign investment, making overseas investments, or engaged in cross-border remittances.

Our FEMA & Foreign Exchange Services

  • FDI Advisory & Structuring: Sector classification, FDI route determination (automatic vs. government route), pricing guidelines under FEMA Valuation Rules, and prohibited sector analysis. Advisory on Press Note 2 (2026) — the revised land-border FDI policy requiring government approval for investors with beneficial ownership in bordering countries.
  • FC-GPR Filing (FDI Inflow): Electronic reporting of share allotment to foreign investors via Form FC-GPR on the FIRMS portal through the AD bank within 30 days of allotment.
  • FC-TRS Filing (Share Transfer): Reporting of transfer of shares between residents and non-residents via Form FC-TRS within 60 days of the date of receipt of consideration or transfer of shares, whichever is earlier.
  • FLA Return (Foreign Liabilities & Assets): Annual return filed directly with the RBI by 15 July each year by all Indian entities that have received FDI or made ODI. Failure to file by this date attracts compounding penalties.
  • ODI Compliance (Overseas Direct Investment): Advisory and filing for Indian entities making overseas investments — Form ODI on FIRMS portal, Annual Performance Report (APR) by 31 December, and ongoing compliance within the 400% net worth limit under the automatic route.
  • External Commercial Borrowings (ECB): Structuring of ECB from eligible foreign lenders, minimum average maturity compliance, end-use monitoring, and monthly reporting (ECB 2 Return) to the RBI via AD bank.
  • LRS (Liberalised Remittance Scheme): Advisory for individual remittances of up to USD 250,000 per financial year for permitted capital and current account transactions.
  • Form 15CA / 15CB: Chartered Accountant certificate (Form 15CB) and taxpayer undertaking (Form 15CA) for remittances to non-residents under Section 195 of the Income Tax Act — filed online on the Income Tax portal before remittance.
  • FEMA Compounding: Preparation and filing of compounding applications before the RBI for past FEMA contraventions — delay in FC-GPR, FLA non-filing, or procedural non-compliances — to regularise violations and avoid prosecution. Late submission fee (LSF) starts at ₹7,500 and is capped under the revised framework.
  • Export-Import FEMA Compliance: Advisory on advance remittances, realisation and repatriation timelines, write-off of unrealised export proceeds, and reporting to AD banks.

Press Note 2 (2026) — Land Border FDI: Issued on 15 March 2026, Press Note 2 amended the land-border FDI policy by introducing a 10% beneficial ownership threshold under PMLA definitions. Any investment where a direct or indirect beneficial owner at 10% or more is a citizen of or entity domiciled in a bordering country now requires prior government approval. This affects investments from entities in China, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Nepal, Myanmar, Bhutan, and Afghanistan — even indirect routes through third-country holding companies. Contact us immediately if your funding involves such structures.
