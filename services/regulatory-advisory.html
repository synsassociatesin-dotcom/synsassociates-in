Regulatory Advisory & Representation

Strategic compliance advisory across India's regulatory landscape — RBI, SEBI, MCA, IRDAI, DPIIT, GST Council, and Income Tax authorities. From compliance gap analysis to show-cause notice responses, we represent your interests before the regulators that matter most to your business.

Why Regulatory Advisory Is a Business-Critical Function

Operating a business in India means engaging with multiple regulators simultaneously — the MCA for corporate compliance, the Income Tax Department for direct taxes, the GST authorities for indirect taxes, the RBI for financial services and foreign exchange, SEBI for capital markets, IRDAI for insurance, and sector-specific bodies like TRAI, DGCA, FSSAI, or PFRDA depending on your industry. The regulatory environment is in a state of constant evolution, with new directions, circulars, and amendments issued throughout the year.

Most businesses discover regulatory gaps only when a notice arrives — at which point the opportunity for a proactive, penalty-free resolution has already passed. SYNS Associates' Regulatory Advisory practice provides a structured approach to continuous compliance: annual gap analysis, real-time regulatory change alerts, and expert representation when enforcement action becomes unavoidable.

Our Regulatory Advisory Services

  • Regulatory Compliance Gap Analysis: Structured review of your existing compliance framework against current regulatory requirements — identifying gaps, non-compliances, and risk areas across all applicable regulators. Delivered as a prioritised remediation report with timelines and responsible functions identified.
  • Show-Cause Notice (SCN) Response: Preparation of detailed, legally sound responses to show-cause notices from MCA, Income Tax, GST, SEBI, RBI, and other regulatory authorities. Includes factual matrix, legal analysis, documentary evidence preparation, and representation at personal hearings.
  • Regulatory Liaison & Correspondence: Managing ongoing correspondence with regulators on behalf of clients — seeking clarifications, filing representations against adverse orders, and engaging with the regulator's designated officers through formal channels.
  • Licensing & Approval Applications: Preparation and filing of applications for sector-specific licences, registrations, and approvals — payment system operator licences (RBI), SEBI intermediary registrations, IRDAI licences, FSSAI licences, and MCA special purpose applications.
  • Regulatory Policy Drafting: Internal policy and compliance manual preparation as required by regulators — AML/CFT policies (RBI/SEBI), information security policies (RBI NBFC IT Framework), data governance frameworks (DPDPA), and risk management frameworks for financial sector entities.
  • Advance Tax Rulings & Opinions: Written legal opinions on complex compliance questions, advance ruling applications before the Authority for Advance Rulings (AAR) — Income Tax and GST — to obtain binding certainty on contentious positions before they are challenged in assessment.
  • Regulatory Change Management: Monthly regulatory updates specific to your industry and compliance profile — covering RBI circulars, SEBI notifications, MCA amendments, GST council decisions, and Income Tax notifications. Subscribers receive action items, not just notifications.
  • Insolvency & NCLT Advisory: Advisory for businesses facing or initiating insolvency proceedings under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), 2016 — creditor petition strategy, corporate insolvency resolution process (CIRP) documentation, and representation before NCLT and NCLAT.
  • Competition Law (CCI) Advisory: Review of proposed mergers, acquisitions, and joint ventures for CCI notification obligations (above the prescribed asset/turnover thresholds), combination filings, and advisory on dominance and anti-competitive practices.
  • Pre-Transaction Regulatory Due Diligence: Comprehensive regulatory diligence for M&A transactions — mapping regulatory approvals required (CCI, RBI, SEBI, sectoral authorities), change-of-control notification obligations, and post-closing compliance transfer timelines.

Our Advisory Approach

  • Proactive, Not Reactive: We establish your full compliance profile from day one and flag regulatory changes before they become compliance failures — not after a notice is received.
  • Commercial Pragmatism: Our regulatory opinions weigh legal risk against commercial reality. We give you a clear risk rating — not just a list of what could theoretically go wrong — so you can make informed business decisions.
  • Regulatory Relationships: Our advocates maintain professional relationships with regulators built on quality submissions, factual accuracy, and timely responses — which translate into more efficient resolution for our clients.
  • Documented Trail: Every regulatory interaction is documented — letters, responses, submissions, and meeting notes — creating an audit trail that demonstrates good-faith compliance efforts.

Settlement Scheme — A Strategic Tool: SEBI's settlement scheme allows market participants to settle certain enforcement proceedings without admission of guilt, paying a scaled settlement amount. Similarly, the Income Tax Department's Vivad se Vishwas scheme has provided a settlement window for pending disputes. Regulatory advisory includes identifying which pending matters are suitable for settlement vs. which should be contested — a judgment call that requires expert knowledge of the specific regulator's settlement practices.
